Subheading 1 (h2)

Jamshedpur is a city located in the state of Jharkhand in India, founded by the late Jamshedji Nusserwanji Tata. Jamshedpur was the first planned city in India, nurtured by Tata Steel.

Subheading 2 (h2)

In 1919 Lord Chelmsford named the city Jamshedpur in honour of its founder, Jamshedji Nausherwanji Tata, whose birthday is celebrated on 3 March as Founder's Day. J. N. Tata had written to his son Dorabji Tata about his vision of a great city in the area. 3rd March is celebrated as the Founders Day and the 225-acre (0.91 km) Jubilee Park is decorated with brilliant light work for about a week, attracting people from all parts of the nation.
